```yaml
    tags: [presentation-skills, public-speaking, engineers, tender-pitch, urgency, executive-presence, confidence, real-outcome]
    summary: |
      Engineers won a tender after 3-4 hours of overnight presentation coaching.
    long: |
      A senior director walked into our office one evening, asking about presentation
      skills training. We told him we had a public class in two weeks. He needed it
      sooner. When? Now. Tonight. Turned out his firm had submitted a major technical
      tender and the client wanted to hear the proposal directly from the engineers
      who would do the work — not from him. The engineers were brilliant technically
      but tongue-tied when speaking. They trembled. They couldn't get the words out.
      He brought them to our office that evening and we coached them for three hours
      — how to stand, where to look, how to pause, how to build a tight slide deck,
      how to fake confidence until it became real. We stayed past four hours getting
      them to practise. The next day they presented well. They won the deal. The
      lesson is small and unglamorous: step-by-step coaching works, everyone feels
      nervous, the skill is in the appearing-confident part — the hands, the eyes,
      the deliberate pause, the slowing down.
    use_when:
      - blog post is about presentation anxiety, public speaking fear, or executive presence
      - blog post is about high-stakes meetings, tender pitches, or client presentations
      - illustrating that confidence is rehearsed, not innate
```
